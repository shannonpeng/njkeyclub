What's DCON?

District Convention, or DCON, is an annual gathering of hundreds of Key Clubbers—from all over the state of New Jersey—to celebrate the end of a successful service year and the beginning of a new one. In 2016, the secret agent-themed DCON will take place at the Ocean Place Resort in Long Branch, NJ, from Friday, April 1st to Sunday, April 3rd.

Throughout the weekend, agents of service will enter contests, attend workshops, receive recognition for their accomplishments, participate in onsite service projects, and elect the next district executive board. There will also be dances held on Friday and Saturday nights—and the beach is right next door!

Check out the official DCON 2016 Trailer below.

Workshops & Service Fair

Want to learn more about the K-Family, fundraising ideas, public speaking, or Key Club's partner service organizations from around the globe? At DCON, you'll attend workshops led by the New Jersey District Board, as well as influential guest speakers. Drop by the Service Fair outside the Atlantic Ballroom to learn more about other volunteer organizations!

Contests & Awards

DCON is a time for honoring Key Club members for their hard work, dedication, talents, and achievements over the course of the service year. Contests include the oratorical and essay contests, and the Talent Show, an audience favorite. Some awards given to clubs and members include the Distinguished Officer Awards, the 100% CMRF Award, the Single Service Award, and the NJ Key Club Scholarships. For more information about contests, contact Division 14 LTG Stephania Gonzalez. For more information about scholarships, contact Division 22 LTG Ciara Clarkin.

Spirit Stick

Which division will claim the coveted Spirit Stick this year? Bring your spirit and division cheers as you represent your division at DCON 2016!

Elections

At DCON, all members will have the opportunity to meet the candidates running for district executive offices for the 2016-2017 service year—District Governor, District Secretary, District Treasurer, and District Editor (the District Webmaster is selected by an application process). Delegates will then represent each club at the House of Delegates to elect the next district executive board! If you are interested in running for a position or have questions about elections, contact Division 5 LTG Zierra Dean.
